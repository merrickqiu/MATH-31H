\section{A linear system over $\mathbb{R}$.}
The system of linear equations can be solved with an augmented matrix.
\begin{align*}
    & \begin{gmatrix}[p]
        1 & 2 & 3 & 1\\
        4 & 5 & 6 & 1\\
        7 & 8 & 9 & 1
        \rowops
        \add[-4]{0}{1}
      \end{gmatrix} \\
    \Rightarrow 
    & \begin{gmatrix}[p]
        1 & 2 & 3 & 1\\
        0 & -3 & -6 & -3\\
        7 & 8 & 9 & 1
        \rowops
        \add[-7]{0}{2}
      \end{gmatrix} \\
    \Rightarrow 
    & \begin{gmatrix}[p]
        1 & 2 & 3 & 1\\
        0 & -3 & -6 & -3\\
        0 & -6 & -12 & -6
        \rowops
        \add[-2]{1}{2}
        \end{gmatrix} \\
    \Rightarrow 
    & \begin{gmatrix}[p]
        1 & 2 & 3 & 1\\
        0 & -3 & -6 & -3\\
        0 & 0 & 0 & 0
        \rowops
        \mult{1}{\scriptstyle \cdot -\frac{1}{3}}
        \end{gmatrix} \\
    \Rightarrow 
    & \begin{gmatrix}[p]
        1 & 2 & 3 & 1\\
        0 & 1 & 2 & 1\\
        0 & 0 & 0 & 0
        \rowops
        \add[-2]{1}{0}
        \end{gmatrix} \\
    \Rightarrow 
    & \begin{gmatrix}[p]
        1 & 0 & -1 & -1\\
        0 & 1 & 2 & 1\\
        0 & 0 & 0 & 0
        \rowops
        \end{gmatrix} \\
  \end{align*}

  The augmented matrix shows that 
  $x = z-1$ and $y = -2z+1$.

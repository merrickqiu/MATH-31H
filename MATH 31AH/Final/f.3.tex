\section*{Problem 3}
We can use gaussian elimination to solve $Ax = 0$.
\begin{align*}
    & \begin{gmatrix}[p]
        1 & 2 & 3 & 4 \\
        0 & 1 & 2 & 3 \\
        1 & 3 & 5 & 7 
        \rowops
        \add[-1]{0}{2}
      \end{gmatrix} \\
     \Rightarrow & \begin{gmatrix}[p]
        1 & 2 & 3 & 4 \\
        0 & 1 & 2 & 3 \\
        0 & 1 & 2 & 3 
        \rowops
        \add[-1]{1}{2}
      \end{gmatrix} \\
    \Rightarrow & \begin{gmatrix}[p]
        1 & 2 & 3 & 4 \\
        0 & 1 & 2 & 3 \\
        0 & 0 & 0 & 0 
        \rowops
        \add[-2]{1}{0}
      \end{gmatrix} \\
    \Rightarrow & \begin{gmatrix}[p]
        1 & 0 & -1 & -2 \\
        0 & 1 & 2 & 3 \\
        0 & 0 & 0 & 0 
      \end{gmatrix} \\
\end{align*}

$Ax = 0$ therefore has basis vectors of 
\[
    \mathcal{B} = \{
    \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ -2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix},
    \begin{bmatrix}2 \\ -3 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}
    \}
\]
